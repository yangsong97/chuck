Wait — what even is a chuck?

All of these critters are marmots, the big-boned members of the ground-squirrel family (genus Marmota). Fun bit: "woodchuck" has nothing to do with wood or chucking — it comes from an Algonquian word like wuchak, and English speakers just squished it into words they recognized. The nickname stuck, so today we've got wood-chucks, rock-chucks, and whistle-pigs galore. Here are all fifteen — search or filter by home continent below.

⚖️ How chonky, exactly?

The Chonk-o-meter

Roughly how heavy each one gets at its chunkiest, in kg. Marmots nearly double their weight before hibernation, so these are late-summer "ready for winter" sizes.

🎬 Which chuck are you?

"How much wood would a woodchuck chuck
if a woodchuck could chuck wood?"

A wildlife biologist once did the maths on the dirt one shifts digging its burrow and joked the answer is about 320 kg — roughly 700 pounds. Woodchucks, of course, chuck exactly zero wood.
